Venus Over Los Angeles

Address: 601 S Anderson Street, Los Angeles CA 90023
Gallerist: Adam Lindemann / 641-653 Imperial Street Holdings LLC
Owner: DUNLIN LLC c/o NCP MANAGEMENT CO

Venus Over Los Angeles was opened in 2015 by multi millionaire real-estate developer and New York blue-chip gallerist Adam Lindemann. For a sense of Lindemann’s background, some family history is in order: His father is George Lindemann, a billionaire oil company executive and a donor to Republican extremists like Michele Bachmann and Newt Gingrich. His brother George Lindemann Jr. was convicted of insurance fraud after setting fire to a live horse.

 

Lindemann is represented by Sheppard Mullin partner Alfred Fraijo Jr., a serial evictor known in Boyle Heights for his involvement in gentrifying non-profits that masquerade as “innovators” in so-called affordable housing. In reality, these organizations—including ELACC, LURN, and From Lot to Spot—collaborate with state agencies and government officials who aid or turn a blind eye to the displacement of low-income tenants (corrupt Councilmember José Huizar, Mayor Eric Garcetti, and LA Metro, to name a few.) 

 

In September 2016, a sea of community residents took to the streets and marched to several galleries, including Venus Over Los Angeles. Gallery-goers mocked the long-time residents and señoras demanding that affluent gallerists and collectors leave their community. The community’s presence clearly upset the privileged crowd, proving their self-imposed alienation from the neighborhood and its needs.

 

Venus Over Los Angeles closed abruptly in September 2017—just a few months after 356 Mission. But Lindemann hasn’t abandoned speculation and gentrification: Within months, he had purchased a $9.75-million building across the river in the Downtown Arts District and filed plans for a 12-story live-work project.
